Sippar was the cult center of the sun god. The city was the high court of Sumer; its divine patron was consequently the ultimate lawgiver.

A remarkable feature of this &#147;Bird Town&#148; was its alignment to the North with Mount Ararat, the most conspicuous natural landmark in the Near East, and to the South at a 45 degree angle with Nippur, the major religious site in pre-Babylonian Mesopotamia.
