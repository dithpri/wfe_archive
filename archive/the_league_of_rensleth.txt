A loose confederation of dictatorial regimes and autocratic monarchies, the League admits nations dotted sporadically around the world that meet &#39;certain governmental and political criteria&#39; into their ranks.

Guided ably by the Tsar of Rislavia and the initial signatories of the Treaty of Rensleth, the League de facto is little more than a mutual non aggression pact that serves to guarantee the &#39;independence of form of governance to all sovereign member nations&#39;.
