Satyameva Jayate (lit.&amp;#8201;&#39;Truth alone triumphs&#39;) is a part of a mantra from the Hindu scripture Mundaka Upanishad. Following the independence of India , it was adopted as the national motto of India on 26 January 1950, the day India became a republic. It is inscribed in the Devanagari script at the base of the Lion Capital of Ashoka and forms an integral, part of the Indian national emblem. The emblem and the words &quot;Satyameva Jayate&quot; are inscribed on one side of all Indian currency

Truth alone triumphs; not falsehood.
Through truth the divine path is spread out
by which the sages whose desires have been completely fulfilled,
reach to where is that supreme treasure of Truth.
