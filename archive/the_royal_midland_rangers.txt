The English army of the 1500s was a transitional force, moving from feudal levies to professional, gunpowder-armed troops. It lacked a permanent standing army, relying instead on commissions of array, regional militias (&quot;Trained Bands&quot;), and mercenaries to raise forces for foreign wars in France or Scotland, often shifting from longbows to guns.
