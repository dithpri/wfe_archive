The Volga is the longest river in Europe. Situated in Russia, it flows through Central Russia to Southern Russia and into the Caspian Sea. The Volga has a length of 3,531 km, and a catchment area of 1,360,000 km[sup]2[/sup]. It is also Europe&#39;s largest river in terms of average discharge at delta &#150; between 8,000 m[sup]3[/sup]/s and 8,500 m[sup]3[/sup]/s &#150; and of drainage basin. It is widely regarded as the national river of Russia. The hypothetical old Russian state, the Rus&#39; Khaganate, arose along the Volga c.&amp;#8201;830 AD. Historically, the river served as an important meeting place of various Eurasian civilizations.
