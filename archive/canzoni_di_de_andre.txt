Fabrizio Cristiano De Andr&eacute; (18 February 1940 &#150; 11 January 1999) was an Italian singer-songwriter and the most-prominent [i]cantautore[/i] of his time. His 40-year career reflects his interests in concept albums, literature, poetry, political protest, and French music. Many public places such as roads, squares, and schools in Italy are named after De Andr&eacute;.
