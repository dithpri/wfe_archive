Libertine: from Latin libertinus &#145;freedman&#146;, from liber &#145;free&#146;. In the mid 16th century, imitating French libertin, the term denoted a member of any of various antinomian sects in France; hence libertine

Nymph: divinities of nature in classical mythology, dwelling in the mountains, forests, trees, and waters.. Generally regarded as personifications of nature, are typically tied to a specific place or landform
