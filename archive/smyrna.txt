Smyrna (/&amp;#712;sm&amp;#604;&amp;#720;rn&amp;#601;/ SMUR-n&amp;#601;; Ancient Greek: &amp;#931;&amp;#956;&amp;#973;&amp;#961;&amp;#957;&amp;#951;, romanized: Sm&yacute;rn&amp;#275;, or &amp;#931;&amp;#956;&amp;#973;&amp;#961;&amp;#957;&amp;#945;, Sm&yacute;rna) is an Ancient Greek city located at a strategic point on the Aegean coast of Asia Minor. Due to its advantageous port conditions, its ease of defence, and its good inland connections, Smyrna rose to prominence.
