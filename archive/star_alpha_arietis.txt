Hamal, also designated as &amp;#945; Arietis (alpha Arietis), is a variable giant star of spectral class K2 in the constellation of Aries. Hamal visual magnitude is 2, making it the 48th brightest star in the sky. Because of its brightness, Hamal is clearly visible to the naked eye when observed from locations with dark skies, and should be also quite easily visible from light polluted areas.
