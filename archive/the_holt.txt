The Holt is an isolated peninsula jutting out into the Varnished sea. measuring roughly 874 square miles; it is surrounded by steep cliffs on every sea-facing side and the only convenient travel or trade routes are overland. Large deciduous forests grow in the cold temperate climate and along with the towering peaks the occasionally dot the landscape serve to make up a majority of this region&amp;#39;s topography.

Although the region is naturally defensible and plentiful in lumber and mineral resources farming is difficult due to centuries of poor agricultural management and the chilly climate. As such the inhabitants of this region rely heavily on trade with neighboring regions to supplement their food supplies.
