[b][region]The Hermitage[/region] [url=https://thehermitage.com/]Museum[/url] located in Davidson County, Tennessee, in the [region]American South[/region] of [region]National Socialist America[/region]

The 1,000 acre, self-sustaining, cotton plantation was owned by Andrew Jackson from 1804 until his death at the Hermitage in 1845. At the time of his death, Jackson owned approximately 150 Africans who lived and acquired training in skilled and field service.

In 1893 the plantation ceased being a Jackson family residence and on December 19, 1960, was designated a National Historic Landmark

The Hermitage was one of the filming locations and settings for the 1955 Disney film [url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2kgcKFbS-LM]Davy Crockett, King of the Wild Frontier[/url]
