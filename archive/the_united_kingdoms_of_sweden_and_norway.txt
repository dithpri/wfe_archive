The [i][b]Union between Sweden and Norway[/b][/i], officially the United Kingdoms of Sweden and Norway, comprises of Sweden and Norway, united under one monarch in a personal union.

Following the Treaty of Kiel and the declaration of Norwegian independence from their previous union with Denmark, a brief war with Sweden resulted in the Convention of Moss and the subsequent Norwegian constitutional revision of 4th November. On the same day, the Norwegian parliament elected [i]Charles XIII[/i] of Sweden as King of Norway.

Thus, the [i][b]United Kingdoms of Sweden and Norway[/b][/i] and her colonies.
