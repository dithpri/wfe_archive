The Federal Territories is a political region in the western hemisphere, formed to bring peace, cooperation, and respect between independent and dependent nations.

The main governing body is the Federal Council, which includes a President elected for a 6-year term and representatives from each member country and territory. The President leads diplomatic efforts and oversees foreign policy, while the Council works on laws, budgets, and resolving conflicts.

The region&#146;s main goals are to promote peace, economic cooperation, human rights, and sustainability. It aims to avoid conflicts by offering diplomatic solutions, fostering economic growth through shared resources, and improving healthcare, education, and equality.
