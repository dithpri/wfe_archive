[color=green]A vast region within the centre of [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasia]Eurasia [/url]spanning multiple climate, from semi-arid desert to forest-steppe to subalpine boreal forest. The [region]Eurasian Steppe[/region] can also be divided into four distinct geocultural regions&amp;mdash;the Pannonian Basin, traditionally home of the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts]Celts[/url] and [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illyrians]Illyrians[/url], the Pontic-Caspian Steppe, home to some [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_slavs]East Slavs[/url], [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cimmerians]Cimmerians[/url], and some [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scytho-Siberian_world]North Aryans[/url], the Central Steppe, home of the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scytho-Siberian_world]North Aryans[/url], and finally the Eastern Steppe, home of the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu]Huns[/url], [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiele_people]Turks[/url], [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donghu]Donghu[/url], and [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sushen]Sushen[/url][/color]
