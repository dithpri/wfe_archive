Jesusland is an Internet meme that emerged after the 2004 U.S. presidential election. It satirizes the red&#150;blue state divide by splitting North America into &#147;The United States of Canada&#148; (more liberal regions) and &#147;Jesusland&#148; (more conservative areas influenced by Christian fundamentalism). The map highlights the political polarization that followed the election, and commentators described it as reflecting the disappointment and division of the time.
