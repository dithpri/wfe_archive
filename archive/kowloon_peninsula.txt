The Kowloon Peninsula forms the southern part of the main landmass in Hong Kong, along the Victoria Harbour and facing towards Hong Kong Island. What is now commonly known as Kowloon includes Kowloon to the south of Boundary Street, and New Kowloon, which is made up of reclaimed land and parts of the New Territories that have been developed into urban areas connected to Kowloon.
