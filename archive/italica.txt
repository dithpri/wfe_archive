The Italian Peninsula is a beautiful geographic location and has seen much history throughout the centuries. It has diverse culture, and the Renaissance adversely affected the region. It is where the Roman Empire started, and Christopher Columbus and Marco Polo were both born in Italy as well. During much of the medieval era, the Italian Peninsula was divided up into smaller nations. The most well known of these is the Most Serene Republic of Venice. Due to being divided and under different banners, the countries were easy pickings for nations like France, Austria, and Aragon.
