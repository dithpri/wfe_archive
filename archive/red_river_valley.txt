Red River Valley of the North
Geography

Location: North America; straddles eastern North Dakota and northwestern Minnesota in the United States, extending north into Manitoba, Canada.

Coordinates: Approximately 47&deg;N 97&deg;W.

Area: Roughly 17,000 sq mi (44,000 sq km).

Terrain: Flat and fertile floodplain formed by the prehistoric glacial Lake Agassiz; among the flattest expanses on Earth.

Elevation: 750&#150;950 ft (229&#150;290 m).

Climate: Continental; cold winters, warm summers, moderate precipitation with spring flood risks due to snowmelt and the northward flow of the Red River.

Natural Hazards: Seasonal flooding is common; region vulnerable to spring floods when meltwater flows north into still-frozen stretches of the Red River.
