The Union of the Arab Maghreb (UMA) is a regional union established on February 17, 1989 in Marrakesh, Morocco, and consists of five countries that collectively represent the western part of the Arab world, namely: Mauritania, Morocco, Algeria, the Sahrawi Arab Republic, Tunisia and Libya. And that by signing the so-called treaty establishing the Arab Maghreb Union. The economies of the five countries are complementary to each other&#146;s economies, as the Union, if activated, will achieve self-sufficiency for all of these countries in most of their needs.
