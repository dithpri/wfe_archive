[color=red]The FMLN was named after rebel leader Farabundo Mart&iacute;, who led the people in an uprising to transform Salvadoran society in 1932. In retaliation, the military regime who had seized power in a 1931 coup, launched &amp;quot;The Massacre&amp;quot;, which saw the killing of some 30,000 people under the guise of being supporters of the insurgency. A good number of those killed were peasants and members of the various indigenous groups that inhabited El Salvador. Thousands of innocent civilians were killed.[/color]
