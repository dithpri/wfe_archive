The history of postage stamps in Great Britain began with the introduction of the Penny Black in 1840, marking the world&#39;s first adhesive postage stamp. Designed by Rowland Hill, the Penny Black featured a profile of Queen Victoria and was issued to simplify and standardize postage rates. It revolutionized the postal system, making prepaid postage accessible to the public.
