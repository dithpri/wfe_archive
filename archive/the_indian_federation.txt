Factbook on The Indian Federation

The Indian Federation, located in South Asia, is a diverse region with a rich history and vibrant culture. It comprises multiple states and union territories, sharing borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. The region experiences varied climates, from tropical in the south to temperate in the north. The Indian Federation gained independence from British colonial rule in 1947 and is now a federal parliamentary democratic republic. It has a diverse economy with key industries including agriculture, manufacturing, services, and information technology. Major languages include Hindi, English, and Bengali; major religions include Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity.
