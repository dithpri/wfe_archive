The [region]Charles Gustav Archipelago[/region] is a part of the Southern rim of Micronesia in the Pacific Ocean. It is situated to the South of the Fiji Archipelago, to the South-East of Vanuatu and New Caledonia, and to the North of New Zealand. 
It was first discovered by Europeans in 1660. The Swedish explorer Ulrik Rosendal named the islands after his king Charles X. Gustav.
