[color=orange]Incheon[/color], or  [color=orange]Inch&#39;&amp;#335;n[/color], literally &quot;kind river,&quot;  formerly Jemulpo or Chemulp&#39;o until the period after 1910, officially the [b]Incheon Metropolitan City[/b], is a city located in northwestern [u]South Korea[/u], bordering Seoul and Gyeonggi to the east. Inhabited since the Neolithic, Incheon was home to just 4,700 people when it became an international port in 1883. Today, about 3 million people live in the city, making it South Korea&#39;s third-most-populous city after Seoul and Busan.
