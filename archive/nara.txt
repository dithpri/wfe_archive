Nara was the capital of Japan during the Nara period from 710 to 794 as the seat of the Emperor before the capital was moved to Kyoto. Nara is home to eight temples, shrines, and ruins, specifically T&amp;#333;dai-ji, Saidai-ji, K&amp;#333;fuku-ji, Kasuga Shrine, Gang&amp;#333;-ji, Yakushi-ji, T&amp;#333;sh&amp;#333;dai-ji, and the Heij&amp;#333; Palace, together with Kasugayama Primeval Forest, collectively form the Historic Monuments of Ancient Nara, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
