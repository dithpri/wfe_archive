The name of a very important region of high hills and low valleys. The word Kadosh has semitic origins, and signifies the Region is considered sacred, and thus intended to denote the elevated character of the citizens and the sublimity of the truths and which distinguish it and its possessors from the other regions. It is said by Ancients, a person bore a token called a Kadosh, to apprise the people that the bearer of this mark was a master and possessed the privilege of &amp;quot;traveling in foreign lands, to earn master&amp;#39;s wages, to better support himself and family, and contribute to the relief of the poor and penniless as he see fit&amp;quot; without the fear of losing his personal liberty.
