The [b]Triangulum Galaxy[/b] is a [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiral_galaxy]spiral galaxy[/url] 2.73 million [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year]light-years[/url] (ly) from Earth in the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constellation]constellation Triangulum[/url]. It is catalogued as [b]Messier 33[/b] or [b]NGC 598[/b]. With the D[sub]25[/sub] isophotal diameter of 18.74 [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsec#Parsecs_and_kiloparsecs]kiloparsecs[/url] (61,100 [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year]light-years[/url]), the Triangulum Galaxy is the third-largest member of the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group]Local Group[/url] of galaxies, behind the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda_Galaxy]Andromeda Galaxy[/url] and the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way]Milky Way[/url].
