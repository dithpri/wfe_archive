In early 1953 during the heat of the Korean War, a group a of warriors known as The Hwarang, following the legacy of the ancient Hwarang Warriors of the 10th Century. They felt threatened by the ongoing violence in the central part of the peninsula and fought there way south east, towards the tip of the peninsula. The Hwarang claimed a small portion of land in the peninsula where they brought there families. They named the region Pyongwong because it meant majestic vast plain. This area is modern day Pyongwong.
