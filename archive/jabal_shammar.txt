The Emirate of Jabal Shammar (Arabic: &amp;#1573;&amp;#1616;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1577; &amp;#1580;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1576;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1604; &amp;#1588;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1617;&amp;#1585;&amp;#8206;), also known as the Emirate of Ha&amp;#702;il or the Rashidi Emirate (&amp;#1573;&amp;#1616;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1577; &amp;#1570;&amp;#1604; &amp;#1585;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1588;&amp;#1616;&amp;#1610;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1583;), is a state in the Arabian Peninsula, including at Najd, existing from 1821 to now. Jabal Shammar in English is translated as the &quot;Mountain of the Shammar&quot;. Jabal Shammar&#39;s capital was Ha&#39;il. It is led by a monarchy of the Rashidi dynasty. It includes parts of modern-day Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Yemen and UAE.
