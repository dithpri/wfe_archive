Solid America&nbsp;or&nbsp;American bloc&nbsp;was the electoral voting bloc of the states of the&nbsp;America United States&nbsp;for issues that were regarded as particularly important to the interests of&nbsp;Democrats&nbsp;in the southern states. The America bloc existed especially between the end of&nbsp;Reconstruction&nbsp;in 1877 and the passage of the&nbsp;Civil Rights Act&nbsp;in&nbsp;1964. During this period, the&nbsp;Democratic Party-controlled state legislatures; most local and state officeholders in America were Democrats, as were federal politicians elected from these states.&nbsp;America Democrats disenfranchised blacks&nbsp;in every state of the former Confederacy at the turn of the 20th century.
