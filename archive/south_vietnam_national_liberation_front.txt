The National Liberation Front of South Vietnam (Vietnamese: M&amp;#7863;t tr&amp;#7853;n D&acirc;n t&amp;#7897;c Gi&amp;#7843;i ph&oacute;ng Mi&amp;#7873;n Nam Vi&amp;#7879;t Nam), also known as the Viet Cong (Vietnamese: Vi&amp;#7879;t C&amp;#7897;ng), was an armed communist political revolutionary organization in South Vietnam and Cambodia. Its military force, the Liberation Army of South Vietnam (LASV), fought under the direction of North Vietnam, against the South Vietnamese and United States governments during the Vietnam War, eventually emerging on the winning side.
