New Hawaii is a tropical ocean region inside the Kohonau pocket dimension, made up of scattered islands, atolls, and active underwater volcanoes. Its climate is warm and humid year round, similar to Earth&#146;s tropical Pacific zones. New land is constantly forming as volcanic activity creates and reshapes islands over time. The ecosystems are rich and ocean driven, with flora and fauna adapted to fast changing coastal environments.
