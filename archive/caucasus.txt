[b]Mount Elbrus is a large, double-coned volcanic mountain in Russia and the highest peak in Europe at 5,642 meters, and the tenth most prominent peak in the world. A dormant volcano, Elbrus forms part of the Caucasus Mountains in Southern Russia, near the border with Georgia. The Caucasus Mountains are defined as the continental divide between Asia and Europe for the region between the Black and Caspian Seas and are a natural border for our people.
