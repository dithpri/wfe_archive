The Great Northern Craton is a large and generally flat area between 39&deg; and 70&deg; N latitude and 15&deg; and 60&deg; E longitude. In the East it is lined with a long Karal mountains. To the Northwest the plains descend into a shallow Baikurian sea, to the Southwest the plains start to ascend into Manzurian hills and Arpanti mountains. In the South there are large lakes in arid and level plains, most notably The Faiaskian sea and the Utral lake. To the North TGNC ends with the coast of the Great Northern Sea. Internally, the Craton is divided into two distinct geographical regions. In the West its the Baikurian plains and in the East its the Great Karalian plains.
