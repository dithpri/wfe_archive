The Kaaba (Arabic: &amp;#1649;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1603;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1593;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1576;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1577;&amp;#8206;, romanized: al-Ka&amp;#703;bah, lit.&amp;#8201;&#39;The Cube&#39;, Arabic pronunciation: [ka&amp;#661;.bah]), also spelled Ka&#39;bah or Kabah, sometimes referred to as al-Ka&amp;#703;bah al-Musharrafah (Arabic: &amp;#1649;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1603;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1593;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1576;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1577; &amp;#1649;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1618;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1615;&amp;#1588;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1617;&amp;#1601;&amp;#1614;&amp;#1577;&amp;#8206;, romanized: al-Ka&amp;#703;bah al-Musharrafah, lit.&amp;#8201;&#39;Honored Ka&#39;bah&#39;), is a building at the center of Islam&#39;s most important mosque, the Masjid al-Haram in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. It is the most sacred site in Islam.
