[b][color=orangered]The Indian Valley Civilization, also known as the Indus Valley Civilization, was a remarkable ancient civilization that thrived around 3300-1300 BCE in the Indus River basin in what is now modern-day Pakistan and northwest India.[/color] [color=grey]It was characterized by advanced urban planning, sophisticated drainage systems, and a script that remains undeciphered.[/color] [color=lavander]Major cities like Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa were centers of trade, culture, and governance. The civilization had a well-structured social hierarchy and relied on agriculture.[/color] [color=green]While its decline remains a subject of debate, factors like environmental changes and invasions may have contributed to its eventual downfall.[/color]
