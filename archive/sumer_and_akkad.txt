Akkad included the cities of Babylon, Cutha, Kish, Akkad, Sippar, and to the north, Opis. Among the cities of Sumer were Eridu, Ur, Lagash, Larsa, Erech, Shuruppak, and on the border, Nippur.

The fish-shaped valley lying between the Euphrates and Tigris rivers, where walled towns were surrounded by green fields and numerous canals flashed in the sunshine, was bounded on the west by the bleak wastes of the Arabian desert. To the east the skyline was fretted by the Iranian Highlands.
