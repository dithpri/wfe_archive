[b][i]Ceuta[/i][/b] is a 18.5 square kilometres (7.1 sq mi) autonomous city of [region]Spain[/region] and an exclave located on the north coast of [region]North Africa[/region] surrounded by [region]Morocco[/region]. Separated from the Iberian peninsula by the Strait of Gibraltar , Ceuta lies on the border of [region]The Mediterranean Sea[/region] and the Atlantic Ocean.
  [b][i]Ceuta[/i][/b] along with the other Spanish exclave Melilla are the only Spanish territories located in mainland [region]Africa[/region]. It was regarded as a part of Cadiz province prior to 14 March 1995, when the city&amp;#39;s Statute of Autonomy was passed.
