Sixteen years ago no more rural village could have been found within five miles of the  West Green. It was as utterly in the country as though situated a hundred miles from civilisation, and by a natural consequence it was country in its ways, habits, and manners. Various lanes leading to it  were rural, which they certainly are not now

No one had yet dreamed of the evil days to come, when mushroom villas should be built upon the ground that not long before was regarded as an irreclaimable morass&#151;when at first a tavern and then a church (the two invariable pioneers of that which, for some unknown reason, we call civilisation) appeared on the scene
