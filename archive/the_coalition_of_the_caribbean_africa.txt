The Coalition of the Caribbean and the African Continent is a vast transoceanic regional bloc linking island and mainland nations through shared economic and cultural interests. Formed to strengthen South-South cooperation, the coalition promotes trade integration, maritime security, and coordinated development planning. Its combined population is diverse, multilingual, and heavily influenced by Afro-Caribbean heritage and traditions. The regional economy relies on a strategic mix of shipping, tourism, natural resources, and emerging technology sectors. Politically, member states retain full sovereignty but participate in a joint council that sets broad regional policy goals.
