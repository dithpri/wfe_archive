Adolf Hitler (German: [&amp;#712;ad&amp;#596;lf &amp;#712;h&amp;#618;tl&amp;#592;] ( listen); 20 April 1889 &#150; 30 April 1945) was a German politician who was the leader of the Nazi Party (Nationalsozialistische Deutsche Arbeiterpartei; NSDAP), Chancellor of Germany from 1933 to 1945, and F&uuml;hrer (&quot;leader&quot;) of Nazi Germany from 1934 to 1945. As dictator of Nazi Germany, he initiated World War II in Europe with the invasion of Poland in September 1939 and was a central figure of the Holocaust.
