The United American Confederation is a confederation of states established in 1776 by thirteen American colonies that had declared their secession from the British Empire. The Confederation States eventually gained independence from Britain in 1783. 

The  Articles of Confederation formed a union of thirteen independent republics - Massachusetts, Pennsylvania, Virginia, Maryland, Delaware, New Jersey, Connecticut, Rhode Island, New Hampshire, North Carolina, South Carolina, New York, and Georgia. A government known as the Congress of Confederation meets in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania to decide on matters on a confederal level. The state governments however deal with political decisions that apply to their respective state.
