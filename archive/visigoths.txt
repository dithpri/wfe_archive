The Visigoths (UK: /&amp;#712;v&amp;#618;z&amp;#618;&amp;#716;&amp;#609;&amp;#594;&amp;#952;s/; US: /&amp;#712;v&amp;#618;z&amp;#618;&amp;#716;&amp;#609;&amp;#593;&amp;#720;&amp;#952;s/, Latin: Visigothi, Wisigothi, Vesi, Visi, Wesi, or Wisi) were the western branches of the nomadic tribes of Germanic peoples referred to collectively as the Goths.[3] These tribes flourished and spread during the late Roman Empire in Late Antiquity, or the Migration Period.
