Samarkand, located in Uzbekistan, is one of the oldest inhabited cities in Central Asia, with a history spanning over 2,500 years. It was a vital hub on the Silk Road, connecting China with the Mediterranean. Known for its stunning Islamic architecture, the city boasts landmarks like Registan Square, a trio of magnificent madrasas; the Shah-i-Zinda necropolis; and the Bibi-Khanym Mosque. It also features the tomb of Timur (Tamerlane), the conqueror who made Samarkand his capital. The city&#146;s rich cultural heritage blends Persian, Turkic, and Islamic influences, earning it a UNESCO World Heritage designation.
