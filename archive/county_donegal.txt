[region]County Donegal[/region] (Irish: [i]Contae Dh&uacute;n na nGall[/i]) is a county of Ireland in the province of Ulster. It is named after the town of Donegal (D&uacute;n na nGall, meaning &#39;fort of the foreigners&#39;) in the south of the county. Donegal County Council is the local council and Lifford the county town.

The population was 159,192 at the 2016 census. It has also been known as (County) Tyrconnell (T&iacute;r Chonaill, meaning &#39;Land of Conall&#39;), after the historic territory of the same name.
