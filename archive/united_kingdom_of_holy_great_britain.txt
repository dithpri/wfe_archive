The air in Vercetti was thick with incense and fear. Pope Francis VII, a gaunt silhouette against the stained glass of his new office, watched a tendril of smoke curl from a faulty electrical socket in the wall. The third time this week. He did not move. Aides scurried, beating at the nascent flame with sacred vestments.



In his smouldering office, the Pope smiled. Everything was proceeding. His little playthings were so beautifully, predictably obedient. All was as it should be in the holy, psychotic empire.
