Georgia (/&amp;#712;d&amp;#658;&amp;#596;&amp;#720;rd&amp;#658;&amp;#601;/ (About this sound listen) JOR-juh) is a state in the southeastern United States. It began as a British colony in 1733, the last of the original Thirteen Colonies. Named after King George II of Great Britain, the Province of Georgia covered the area from South Carolina down to Spanish Florida and New France along Louisiana (New France), also bordering to the west towards the Mississippi River. Georgia was the fourth state to ratify the United States Constitution, on January 2, 1788.[/b][/color]
