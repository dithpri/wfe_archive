Karelia (Karelian, Finnish and Estonian: Karjala; Russian: &amp;#1050;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1077;&amp;#1083;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1103;, Kareliya; Swedish: Karelen), the land of the Karelian peoples, is an area in Northern Europe of historical significance for Finland, Russia, and Sweden. It is currently divided between the Russian Republic of Karelia, the Russian Leningrad Oblast, and Finland (the regions of South Karelia and North Karelia).
