Compared to Washington D.C. the Mojave survived the Great War relatively unscathed, but not for lack of trying on the part of the Chinese. At the culmination of the Sino-American War, 77 atomic warheads were launched at the city of Las Vegas and the surrounding areas, but due to intricate defense systems set in place by wealthy businessman Mr. House, only nine struck the surrounding desert.

In the years since, the Mojave has largely been ignored by the developing post-apocalyptic civilizations.
