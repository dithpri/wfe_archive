The [b]North American Free Trade Agreement[/b] (NAFTA) is an agreement signed by Canada, Mexico, and the United States, creating a trilateral trade bloc in North America. The agreement came into force on January 1, 1994. It superseded the Canada&#150;United States Free Trade Agreement between the U.S. and Canada.
