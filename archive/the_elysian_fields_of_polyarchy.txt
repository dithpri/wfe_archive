The Elysian Fields are, according to Homer, located on the western edge of the Earth by the stream of Okeanos. In the time of the Greek oral poet Hesiod, the Elysian Fields would also come to be known as the Fortunate Isles or the Isles (or Islands) of the Blessed, located in the western ocean at the end of the earth. The Thebean poet Pindar, describing them as having shady parks, with residents indulging their athletic and musical pastimes.
