The Ember Pacific Isles is a volcanic archipelago region located across the Southern Pacific Ocean, where lush greenlands meet glowing volcanic coasts. Known for its striking contrast of nature and fire, the region blends rich ocean ecosystems with powerful geothermal landscapes.

The islands are covered in dense fern forests, coastal cliffs, and volcanic terrain shaped by ongoing geothermal activity. Black sand beaches and warm mineral waters are common across the region.

Ember Pacific Isles is part of the wider Oceania spirit, representing island unity, independence, and cooperation between nations across the sea.

The region operates as a loose federation where nations maintain sovereignty while sharing cultural and environmental identity.
