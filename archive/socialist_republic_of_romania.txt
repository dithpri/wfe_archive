The Socialist Republic of Romania (Romanian: Republica Socialist&amp;#259; Rom&acirc;nia, RSR) was a Marxist&#150;Leninist one-party communist state that existed officially in Romania from 1947 to 1989. From 1947 to 1965, the state was known as the Romanian People&#39;s Republic (Republica Popular&amp;#259; Rom&icirc;n&amp;#259;, RPR). The country was an Eastern Bloc state and a member of the Warsaw Pact with a dominant role for the Romanian Communist Party enshrined in its constitutions. Geographically, Romania bordered the Black Sea to the east; the Soviet Union (via the Ukrainian and Moldavian SSRs) to the north and east; Hungary and Yugoslavia to the west and Bulgaria to the south.
