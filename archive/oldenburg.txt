Oldenburg[/region] city-state is situated at the Rivers Hunte and Haaren, in the northwestern region of [region]Niedersachsen[/region] between the cities of [region]Free Hanseatic City of Bremen[/region] in the east and Groningen (Netherlands) in the west.  

On May 29, 1932, the NSDAP received 48.4% of the state election, enough to put the National-Socialist party in charge of forming a state government and, significantly, making Oldenburg the first state in the country to put the National-Socialists in power based on electoral turnout.[/b]
