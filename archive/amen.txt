[hr][b] Amen (Hebrew: &amp;#1488;&amp;#1464;&amp;#1502;&amp;#1461;&amp;#1503;, &amp;#702;&amp;#257;m&amp;#275;n; Ancient Greek: &amp;#7936;&amp;#956;&amp;#942;&amp;#957;, am&ecirc;n; Arabic: &amp;#1570;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1740;&amp;#1606;, &amp;#257;m&amp;#275;n; Aramaic/Classical Syriac: &amp;#1808;&amp;#1825;&amp;#1821;&amp;#1826;, &#39;am&amp;#299;n) is an Abrahamic declaration of affirmation first found in the Hebrew Bible, and subsequently in the New Testament. It is used in Jewish, Christian and Islamic worship, as a concluding word, or as a response to a prayer.[/b][hr]
