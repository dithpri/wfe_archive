The [b]Small Magellanic Cloud (SMC)[/b] is a [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarf_galaxy]dwarf galaxy[/url] near the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way]Milky Way[/url]. Classified as a dwarf [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irregular_galaxy]irregular galaxy[/url], the SMC has a D[sub]25[/sub] isophotal diameter of about 5.78 kiloparsecs (18,900 light-years), and contains several hundred million stars. It has a total mass of approximately 7 billion [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_mass]solar masses[/url]. At a distance of about 200,000 [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year]light-years[/url], the SMC is among the nearest intergalactic neighbors of the Milky Way and is one of the most distant objects visible to the [url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naked_eye]naked eye[/url].
