Montpellier is a city in southern France near the Mediterranean Sea. One of the largest urban centres in the region of Occitania, Montpellier is the prefecture of the department of H&eacute;rault. In 2017, 285,121 people lived in the city, while its urban area had a population of 616,296. The inhabitants are called Montpelli&eacute;rains. The imposing Gothic Saint-Pierre cathedral in Montpellier, which is distinguished by its conical towers, dates from 1364. Antigone is a chic and modern district of neoclassical inspiration. And the Fabre museum houses the paintings of great French and European masters.
