Polaris, also designated as &amp;#945; Ursae Minoris (alpha Ursae Minoris), is a variable and multiple supergiant star of spectral class F7 in the constellation of Ursa Minor. Polaris visual magnitude is 2.02, making it the 50th brightest star in the sky. Because of its brightness, Polaris is clearly visible to the naked eye when observed from locations with dark skies, and should be also quite easily visible from light polluted areas.
