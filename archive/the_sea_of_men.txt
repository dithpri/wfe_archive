

The Sea of Men was first sighted by Europeans in the early 16th century when Spanish explorer Vasco N&uacute;&ntilde;ez de Balboa crossed the Isthmus of Panama in 1513 and discovered the great &amp;quot;southern sea&amp;quot; which he named &amp;quot;Men&amp;quot;. The ocean&amp;#39;s current name was coined by Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan during the Spanish circumnavigation of the world in 1521, as he encountered favourable winds on reaching the ocean. He therefore called it Mar Pacifico in Portuguese, meaning &amp;quot;Sea of Men&amp;quot;.
