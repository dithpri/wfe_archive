[b]Elohim[/b] (Hebrew: &amp;#1488;&amp;#1457;&amp;#1500;&amp;#1465;&amp;#1492;&amp;#1460;&amp;#1497;&amp;#1501;, romanized: &amp;#702;&amp;#276;l&amp;#333;h&amp;#299;m: [(&amp;#660;)elo&amp;#712;(h)im]), the plural of &amp;#1488;&amp;#1457;&amp;#1500;&amp;#1493;&amp;#1465;&amp;#1492;&amp;#1463;&amp;#1468;&amp;#8206; (&amp;#702;&amp;#276;l&amp;#333;ah), is a Hebrew word meaning &quot;gods&quot; or &quot;godhood&quot;. Although the word is plural, in the Hebrew Bible it most often takes singular verbal or pronominal agreement and refers to a single deity, particularly the God of Israel in the majestic plural.
