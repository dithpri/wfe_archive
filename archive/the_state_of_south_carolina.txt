The State of South Carolina is home of the first American colony and birthplace of The Republic of United States of America. The small settlement which is the earliest recorded settlement on the North American continent, was known as the Colony of Carolopolis, which later became known as the Settlement of Charles Towne, and then the City of Charleston before becoming a commonwealth and reverting back to the original name of Carolopolis. After the war that became known as the 2nd American Revolution, The State of South Carolina relocated the states capitol city from Columbia to Carolopolis which was originally the capitol when the city was first named Charleston, becoming The Commonwealth of Carolopolis.
