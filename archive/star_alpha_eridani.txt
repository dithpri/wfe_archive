Achernar, also designated as &amp;#945; Eridani (alpha Eridani), is a variable main-sequence star of spectral class B3 in the constellation of Eridanus. Achernar visual magnitude is 0.46, making it the 9th brightest star in the sky. Because of its brightness, Achernar is clearly visible to the naked eye when observed from locations with dark skies, and should be also quite easily visible from light polluted areas.
