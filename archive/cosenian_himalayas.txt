The Himalayas, or Himalaya (/&amp;#716;h&amp;#618;m&amp;#601;&amp;#712;le&amp;#618;&amp;#601;, h&amp;#618;&amp;#712;m&amp;#593;&amp;#720;l&amp;#601;j&amp;#601;/), (Sanskrit: him&aacute; (&amp;#2361;&amp;#2367;&amp;#2350;, &quot;snow&quot;) and &amp;#257;-laya (&amp;#2310;&amp;#2354;&amp;#2351;, &quot;abode, receptacle, dwelling&quot;)), is a mountain range in Asia separating the plains of the Indian subcontinent from the Tibetan Plateau. The range has many of Earth&#39;s highest peaks, including the highest, Mount Everest, at the border between Nepal and China. The Himalayas include over fifty mountains exceeding 7,200 m (23,600 ft) in elevation, including ten of the fourteen 8,000-metre peaks. By contrast, the highest peak outside Asia (Aconcagua, in the Andes) is 6,961 m (22,838 ft) tall.
