[b]The purpose of the Iliad is not simply to glorify war and killing, but it is also to show precisely what it destroys. In the height of its excellence, war destroys what is noble, virtuous, dear, and long-standing. And, in this way, the poet honors all the gods -- including Hera and Athena -- and not just Ares and Aphrodite.
