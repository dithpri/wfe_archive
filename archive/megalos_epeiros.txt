Pyrrhus of Epeiros ruled the ancient kingdom of Epeiros from 306-302 BC and 297-272 BC, he claimed the Macedonian throne in two ocasions 288-285 BC and 274-272 BC and was also Tyrant of Syracuse 278-276 BC. He invaded Italy and defeated the Romans in Heraclea and Asculum, after the inconclusive battle of Beneventum he was forced to retreat, he was finally killed in the battle for Argos. Due to heavy loss in the war in Italy the term Pyrric victory come into existence.
