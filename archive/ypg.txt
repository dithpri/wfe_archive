[b]The People&#39;s Protection Units (Kurdish: Yek&icirc;ney&ecirc;n Parastina Gel&amp;#8206;,&amp;#1740;&amp;#1749;&amp;#1705;&amp;#1740;&amp;#1606;&amp;#1749;&amp;#1705;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1606;&amp;#1740; &amp;#1662;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1587;&amp;#1578;&amp;#1606;&amp;#1740; &amp;#1711;&amp;#1749;&amp;#1604; pronounced [j&amp;#593;kin&aelig;jen p&amp;#593;r&amp;#593;stin&amp;#593; g&aelig;l]; YPG), also known as the People&#39;s Defense Units, is the main armed service of the Federation of Northern Syria &#150; Rojava. The YPG is primarily ethnic Kurdish, but it also recruits Arabs, Turks, and Westerners. The Syriac Military Council are Assyrian/Syriac Christian units integrated into its command structure.

The YPG considers itself a democratic people&#39;s army and conducts internal elections as a method of appointing officers.[/b]
