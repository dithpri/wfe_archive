[b]Czechoslovakia[/b] was a former country in Central Europe, established in 1918 after World War I by the unification of the Czech and Slovak regions. It underwent several political changes during the 20th century, including Nazi occupation and becoming a socialist state during the Cold War. In 1993, Czechoslovakia peacefully split into two independent countries: the [b]Czech Republic[/b] and [b]Slovakia[/b], in an event known as the &quot;Velvet Divorce.&quot;
