[b][color=brown]Ghazni is a city in central Afghanistan strategically located along Highway 1, which has served as the main road between Kabul and Kandahar for thousands of years. Situated on a plateau at 2,219 meters above sea level, the city is 150 km south of Kabul and is the capital of Ghazni Province. During the First Anglo-Afghan War in the 19th century, Ghazni was partially destroyed by British-Indian forces. 

In August 2018 the city became of the site of the Battle of Ghazni with the [region]Global Liberation Army[/region] occupying it[/color]
