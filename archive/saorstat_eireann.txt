on December 6th, 1921 the Anglo-Irish treaty partitioned the emerald isle into two political entities with 26 counties in the predominately Roman Catholic south becoming known as the Irish Free State and 6 predominately Protestant counties under the name of Northern Ireland opted to remain a functioning member of the United Kingdom. 

President:William Cosgrave 
Prime Minister:Eamon De Valera
