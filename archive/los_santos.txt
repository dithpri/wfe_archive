[b][color=blue]At four thousand eighty-three square miles, Los Santos is more like a small planet than a metropolis. Its geography encompasses majestic shoreline and hard-bitten desert, forested mountainsides and fathomless cement flatlands, eight-lane freeways and desolate dirt highways frequented primarily by lizards and tumbleweeds. Movie stars, migrant workers, and criminals have an equal stake in the cosmology of Los Santos. [/b][/color]
