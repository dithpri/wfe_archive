The&nbsp;Sombrero Galaxy&nbsp;(also known as&nbsp;Messier Object 104,&nbsp;M104&nbsp;or&nbsp;NGC 4594) is a&nbsp;peculiar galaxy&nbsp;of unclear&nbsp;classification[5]&nbsp;in the&nbsp;constellation&nbsp;borders of&nbsp;Virgo&nbsp;and&nbsp;Corvus, being about 9.55&nbsp;megaparsecs&nbsp;(31.1&nbsp;million&nbsp;light-years)&nbsp;from the Milky Way galaxy. It is a member of the&nbsp;Virgo II Groups, a series of galaxies and&nbsp;galaxy clusters&nbsp;strung out from the southern edge of the&nbsp;Virgo Supercluster.&nbsp;It has a diameter of approximately 15 kiloparsecs (49,000 light-years),[7]&nbsp;three-tenths the size of the&nbsp;Milky Way.
