A Rajput (from Sanskrit raja-putra, &#147;son of a king&#148;) is a member of one of the patrilineal clans of The Undivided Bharat. They are descendants of ruling Hindu warrior classes of North India. Rajputs rose to prominence during the 6th to 12th centuries.
