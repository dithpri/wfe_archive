[color=d4af37][b]Empire of Brazil[/b][/color]

[color=darkgreen]The [u]Empire of Brazil[/u] was a state that broadly comprised the territories which form modern Brazil and [region]Uruguay[/region]. Its government was a representative parliamentary constitutional monarchy under the rule of Emperors Pedro and his son [nation=short]Pedro II[/nation], both members of the [nation=short]House of Braganza[/nation]. A colony of Portugal, Brazil became the seat of the Portuguese Empire in 1808, when the Portuguese Prince regent, later King Jo&atilde;o VI, fled from Napoleon I&amp;#39;s invasion of Portugal and established himself and his government in [region=Rio de Janeiro]. Jo&atilde;o VI later returned to Portugal, leaving his eldest son and heir, Pedro, to rule Brazil as regent.[/color]
