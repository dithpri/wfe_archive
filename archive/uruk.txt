In this city of southern Mesopotamia, Sumerian civilization seems to have reached its creative peak. Its districts have the first monumental constructions in history. Much of Near Eastern architecture can trace it roots to these prototypical buildings.

Called [b][i]Orchoe[/i][/b] by the Greco-Macedonians, the ancient city prospered under Hellenistic rule with new temples built. The priests were able to preserve their celestial observations on clay tablets, fortunately for the future of science and astronomy.

The Arabic name of Babylonia, [i]al-Iraq[/i], was thought to be derived from the name of [i]Uruk[/i].
