In Greek mythology, Persephone (/p&amp;#601;r&amp;#712;s&amp;#603;f&amp;#601;ni/, per-SEH-f&amp;#601;-nee; Greek: &amp;#928;&amp;#949;&amp;#961;&amp;#963;&amp;#949;&amp;#966;&amp;#972;&amp;#957;&amp;#951;), also called Kore (/&amp;#712;k&amp;#596;&amp;#601;ri&amp;#720;/; &quot;the maiden&quot;) or Cora, is the daughter of Zeus and the harvest goddess Demeter, and is the queen of the underworld. Homer describes her as the formidable, venerable majestic princess of the underworld, who carries into effect the curses of men upon the souls of the dead.
