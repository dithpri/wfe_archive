Mountainous, pale, &quot;rather bracing&quot; if one is being optimistic, Greater Ghalea encompasses a relatively quiet patch of taiga giving way at times to tundra in which a handful of nations have established foundations. The earliest, Shirogane, has devoted prodigious resources to the &quot;optimization&quot; of wide swathes of boreal countryside, effectively clearing the way for the appearance of later neighbors. A modest yet capable peacekeeping force comprised of internationally-supplied regiments serves to secure the borders (more or less), more out of a prudent interest in knowing what, precisely, is lurking in the frigid wastes than out of any exceptional xenophobia.
