The United Nations is an intergovernmental organization established in 1945 to promote international cooperation and maintain international peace and security. It is comprised of 193 member states and plays a critical role in addressing global issues such as poverty, climate change, and human rights abuses.

How did United Nations Start?

The United Nations was created following World War II in 1945 by representatives from 50 countries who gathered in San Francisco to sign the United Nations Charter. The key players in the creation of the UN were the United States, the United Kingdom, China, the Soviet Union, and France, known as the &quot;Big Five.&quot;
