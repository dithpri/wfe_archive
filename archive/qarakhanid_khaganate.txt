The [b]Qarakhanid Khaganate[/b] (840&#150;1212) was a Turkic confederation that ruled vast territories in Central Asia. Notable as the first Turkic dynasty to officially convert to Islam in the mid-10th century, they fundamentally shifted the region&#146;s demographic and cultural identity from Iranian to Turkic while blending nomadic traditions with Perso-Arab Islamic urban culture. At their peak, they conquered the Samanid Empire and controlled major Silk Road hubs like Samarkand, Bukhara, and Kashgar, fostering a &quot;Turko-Islamic&quot; renaissance in architecture and literature&#151;most famously seen in the Kalyan Minaret and the literary work Kutadgu Bilig.
