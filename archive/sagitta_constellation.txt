[i]Sagitta is a dim but distinctive constellation in the northern sky. Its name is Latin for &#39;arrow&#39;, not to be confused with the significantly larger constellation Sagittarius &#39;the archer&#39;. It was included among the 48 constellations listed by the 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy, and it remains one of the 88 modern constellations defined by the International Astronomical Union.[/i][b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025.[/b]
