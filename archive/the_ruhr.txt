The Occupation of [region]The Ruhr[/region] by France and Belgium between 10 January 1923 and 1925 was in response to the Weimar Republic&#39;s failure to continue its reparation payments in the aftermath of World War I. [region]The Ruhr[/region] river valley in western Germany, is the centre of German bituminous coal, iron and steel production. Before 1918 much of the iron ore used in steel production came from German-occupied Lorraine

[region]The Ruhr[/region] is bordered by the rivers Ruhr River to the south, Rhine River to the west, and Lippe to the north[/b]
