The Isthmus of Egg Plague is a narrow, white-dusted land bridge that would be hauntingly beautiful if it weren&#39;t so incredibly annoying. While scholars once feared the calcified eggs held world-ending horrors, it turns out the plague caused by the eggs found on the isthmus is purely psychological. The eggs are biologically programmed to be sentient, petty, and obsessed with acoustic mockery. If you trip or sneeze, every egg within a fifty-yard radius will perfectly mimic the sound, but slightly louder and in a mocking, nasally tone. This creates a humiliating echo that can follow a traveler for miles across the craggy, yolk-stained terrain.
