Encompassing an area of 619.57 km2 (239.22 sq mi) and with a population of 13,484,462 as of 2020,[5] it is composed of sixteen highly urbanized cities: the capital city, Manila, Caloocan, Las Pi&ntilde;as, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Marikina, Muntinlupa, Navotas, Para&ntilde;aque, Pasay, Pasig, Quezon City, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, along with one independent municipality, Pateros. As the second most populous and the most densely populated region in the Philippines, it ranks as the 9th most populous metropolitan area in Asia and the 6th most populous urban area in the world.
