[b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025![/b][i]Bo&ouml;tes: is a constellation in the northern sky, located between 0&deg; and +60&deg; declination, and 13 and 16 hours of right ascension on the celestial sphere. The name comes from Latin: Bo&amp;#333;t&amp;#275;s, which comes from Ancient Greek: &amp;#914;&amp;#959;&amp;#974;&amp;#964;&amp;#951;&amp;#962;, romanized: Bo&amp;#7763;t&amp;#275;s &#39;herdsman&#39; or &#39;plowman&#39; (literally, &#39;ox-driver&#39;; from &amp;#946;&amp;#959;&amp;#8166;&amp;#962; bo&ucirc;s &#39;cow&#39;). One of the 48 constellations described by the 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy, Bo&ouml;tes is now one of the 88 modern constellations. It contains the fourth-brightest star in the night sky, the orange giant Arcturus.[/i]
