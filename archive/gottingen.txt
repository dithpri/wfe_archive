G&ouml;ttingen is a university town and district Capital in [region]Niedersachsen[/region] (Lower Saxony), Germany.  The River Leine runs through the town.

German Chancellors Otto von Bismarck and Gerhard Schr&ouml;der attended law school at the G&ouml;ttingen University.
G&ouml;ttingen is noted for its production of optical and precision-engineered machinery, being the seat of the light microscopy division of Carl Zeiss, Inc., and a main site for Sartorius AG which specialises in bio-technology and measurement equipment[/b]
