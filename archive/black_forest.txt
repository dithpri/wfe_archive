[region]Black Forest[/region] is a large forested mountain range in the southwest of [region]The Empire of Germany[/region] bounded by the Rhine valley to the west and south. [region]Black Forest[/region] is the highest part of the South German Scarplands and much of it is densely wooded, a fragment of the Hercynian Forest of Antiquity. Its highest peak is the Feldberg with an elevation of 1,493 metres. From north to south the [region]Black Forest[/region] extends for over 160 km, attaining a width of up to 50 kilometres in the south, and up to 30 kilometres in the north. It is at the [region]Trainingslager[/region] deep in the range where our [region]Wehrmacht of the Reich[/region] are trained.[/b]
