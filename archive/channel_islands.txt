[b]The Channel Islands[/b] are located in the English Channel, near the coast of Normandy, France, and consist of several small islands, mainly including [b]Jersey[/b], [b]Guernsey[/b], [b]Alderney[/b], and [b]Sark[/b]. English is the main official language, although some areas still retain influences from Norman French. The economy is based mainly on financial services, tourism, and agriculture, and is especially well known for its dairy products and seafood.
