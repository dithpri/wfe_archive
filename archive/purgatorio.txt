[b][color=indigo]Purgatorio (Italian for &amp;quot;Purgatory&amp;quot;) is the second part of Dante&amp;#39;s Divine Comedy, following the Inferno, and preceding the Paradiso.[/b][/color]

[color=purple][i]In the poem, Purgatory is depicted as a mountain in the Southern Hemisphere, consisting of a bottom section (Ante-Purgatory), seven levels of suffering and spiritual growth (associated with the seven deadly sins), and finally the Earthly Paradise at the top.[/color][/i]
