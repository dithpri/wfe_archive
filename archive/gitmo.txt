The [B]Guantanamo Bay detention camp[/B] ([URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language]Spanish[/URL]: [I]Centro de detenci&oacute;n de la bah&iacute;a de Guant&aacute;namo[/I]) is a United States [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_prison]military prison[/URL] within the [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_Naval_Base]Guantanamo Bay Naval Base[/URL], also referred to as [B]Gitmo[/B] ([URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English]/&amp;#712;&amp;#609;&amp;#618;tmo&amp;#650;/[/URL] [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key][I]GIT-moh[/I][/URL]), on the coast of [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay]Guant&aacute;namo Bay[/URL] in [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba]Cuba[/URL]. As of May 2024, of the 779 people detained there since January 2002 when the military prison first opened after the [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/September_11_attacks]September 11 attacks[/URL], 740 had been transferred elsewhere, 30 remained there, and nine had [URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_in_custody]died while in custody[/URL].[sup][URL=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_detention_camp#cite_note-1][1][/URL][/sup]
