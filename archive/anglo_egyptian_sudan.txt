The Anglo-Egyptian Sudan was a condominium of the United Kingdom and Egypt between 1899 and 1956, corresponding to the territory of what is now both Sudans and parts of southeastern Libya. Established after the Anglo-Egyptian conquest, it was officially governed by a British-nominated Governor-General, though effective control was held by the British. The regime operated as a de facto colonial administration, focusing development in the north and establishing a separate, distinct administration for the south.
