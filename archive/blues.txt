Blues is a music genre[3] and musical form that originated among African Americans in the Deep South of the United States around the 1860s.[2] Blues has incorporated spirituals, work songs, field hollers, shouts, chants, and rhymed simple narrative ballads from the African-American culture. The blues form is ubiquitous in jazz, rhythm and blues, and rock and roll, and is characterized by the call-and-response pattern, the blues scale, and specific chord progressions, of which the twelve-bar blues is the most common. Blue notes (or &quot;worried notes&quot;), usually thirds, fifths or sevenths flattened in pitch, are also an essential part of the sound.
