The Crime Infested Region of South Detroit used to be a wealthy and prosperous land. That is, until the great economic crash of 2008 descended upon its once peaceful citizens like a storm of locusts on a field of booming crops in the dust bowl. Ever since, the land, and the people in it, has degenerated into scattered shantytowns rampant with poverty. The people live in constant fear, afraid of being robbed, murdered, or molested at a whim&amp;#39;s notice. 

Will the blacks ever return to their former glory in the great automotive boom of the early 20th century?
