[u]The Democratic Republic of Afghanistan (DRA), covers the period when the People&amp;#39;s Democratic Party of Afghanistan (PDPA) ruled the country.[/u]

The PDPA came to power through a revolution, which ousted the unpopular government of Mohammad Daoud Khan. Daoud was succeeded by Nur Muhammad Taraki as head of state and government on 30 April 1978.

Taraki, the organizer of the Saur Revolution, introduced several revolutionary reforms during his rule, the most notable being equal rights to women, universal education, land reform, public utilities and infrastructure.
