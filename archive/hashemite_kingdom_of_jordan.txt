Jordan (/&amp;#712;d&amp;#658;&amp;#596;&amp;#720;rd&amp;#601;n/; Arabic: &amp;#1575;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1571;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1583;&amp;#1606;&amp;#8206; Al-Urdun), officially the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan (Arabic: &amp;#1575;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1603;&amp;#1577; &amp;#1575;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1571;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1583;&amp;#1606;&amp;#1610;&amp;#1577; &amp;#1575;&amp;#1604;&amp;#1607;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1588;&amp;#1605;&amp;#1610;&amp;#1577;&amp;#8206; Al-Mamlakah Al-Urduniyah Al-Hashimiyah[needs IPA]), is an Arab kingdom in Western Asia, on the East Bank of the Jordan River. Jordan is bordered by Saudi Arabia to the east and south, Iraq to the north-east, Syria to the north, and Israel and Palestine to the west. Jordan has a strategic location at the crossroads of Asia, Africa and Europe. Inhabited since the Paleolithic era rich in remains with evidence of human inhabitance by Homo erectus, Neanderthal and later modern humans. The Kharanah area in eastern Jordan has evidence of human huts from about 20,000 years ago or may be older.
