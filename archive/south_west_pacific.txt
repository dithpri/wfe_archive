South West Pacific Area (SWPA) was the name given to the Allied supreme military command in the South West Pacific Theater of World War II. It was one of four major Allied commands in the Pacific War. SWPA included the Philippines, Borneo, the Dutch East Indies (excluding Sumatra), East Timor, Australia, the Territories of Papua and New Guinea, and the western part of the Solomon Islands. General Douglas MacArthur was appointed as the Supreme Commander, Southwest Pacific Area, on its creation on 18 April 1942. SWPA was abolished on 2 September 1945 but it is liberated and reestablished by the Free Trade Zone Forces on 17.04.2016.
