The Boer Republics were independent, self-governing states established in the 19th century by Dutch-speaking settlers (Boers/Afrikaners) in South Africa, most notably the South African Republic (Transvaal) and the Orange Free State. Formed during the Great Trek to escape British rule in the Cape Colony, these Calvinist republics existed until 1902.
