The Boer Republics were independent, Dutch-speaking (Afrikaans) states established in the 19th century by Boer settlers (Voortrekkers) in South Africa, most notably the South African Republic (Transvaal) and the Orange Free State. Founded during the &quot;Great Trek&quot; to escape British rule in the Cape Colony, these republics sought autonomy but were annexed by Britain following the Second Boer War (1899&#150;1902), eventually forming part of the Union of South Africa.
