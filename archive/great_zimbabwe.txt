Proto-Shona speaking societies first emerged in the middle Limpopo valley in the 9th century and move on to the Zimbabwean highlands. The Zimbabwean plateau became the centre of subsequent Shona states, in the 10th century &amp; Kingdom Mapungubwe in the 11th century. Then Shona civilisations that would dominate the region during the 13th to 15th centuries, evidenced by ruins at Great Zimbabwe, near Masvingo, and other smaller sites. They used unique dry stone architecture.The Kingdom of Mapungubwe was the first in a series of sophisticated trade states developed in Zimbabwe by the time of the first European explorers from Portugal came . From about 1300 until 1600, Mapungubwe was eclipsed by the Kingdom of Zimbabwe.
