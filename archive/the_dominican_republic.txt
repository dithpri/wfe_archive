The Dominican Republic is a nation on the island of Hispaniola, part of the Greater Antilles archipelago in the Caribbean region. The western three-eighths of the island is occupied by the nation of Haiti, making Hispaniola one of two Caribbean islands, along with Saint Martin, that are shared by two countries. Its capital city is Santo Domingo.

It is the most visited destination in the Caribbean. As the Caribbean&amp;#39;s most geographically diverse nation, the Dominican Republic is home to the region&amp;#39;s tallest mountain peak, Pico Duarte, as well as the Caribbean&amp;#39;s largest lake and lowest elevation, Lake Enriquillo.
