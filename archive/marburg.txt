[region]Marburg[/region] is the seat of the oldest Protestant-founded university in the world, the University of Marburg (Philipps-Universit&auml;t-Marburg), founded in 1527. It is one of the smaller &amp;quot;university towns&amp;quot; in Germany: Greifswald, [region]Erlangen[/region], [region]Jena[/region], and T&uuml;bingen, as well as the city of Gie&szlig;en, which is located 30 km south of Marburg.

In 1529, Philipp I of Hesse arranged the Marburg Colloquy, to propitiate Martin Luther and Huldrych Zwingli.[/b]
