1. *Europe*:
Western Rome was mainly located in the western part of the European continent. Its main territories included modern-day Italy, France, Spain, Portugal and parts of England and Germany. Rome, the capital, was strategically located in central Italy, along the Tiber River, providing a center of administration and governance. The Italian peninsula served as the heart of the empire, the most important economic and cultural center.
