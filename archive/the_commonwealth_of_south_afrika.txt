[region]The Commonwealth of South Afrika[/region] is one of the oldest existing customs unions. 

Following the Second Boer War, and the establishment of British control over the Boer republics, the Commonwealth of South Africa was formed in 1903 with the signing of the Petroria Convention. It consists of Cape Colony, Basutoland, the Bechuanaland Protectorate, Natal, the Orange Free State, the[nation]Traansvaal[/nation], and [nation]Confederate Rhodesia[/nation]. Swaziland joined in 1904.

Its aim is to maintain the free trade between member countries and it provides for a common external tariff to this common customs area. All tariffs collected in the common customs area are paid into the Petroria National Revenue Fund. The revenue is then shared among all member states.
