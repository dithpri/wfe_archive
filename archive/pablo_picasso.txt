[b]Pablo Ruiz Picasso[/b] (25 October 1881 &#150; 8 April 1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker and ceramicist who spent most of his adult life in France. Regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, among his most famous works are the proto-Cubist Les Demoiselles d&#39;Avignon (1907), and Guernica (1937), a dramatic portrayal of the bombing of Guernica by the German and Italian airforces during the Spanish Civil War.

In 1944, Picasso joined the French Communist Party, attended the World Congress of Intellectuals in Defense of Peace in Poland, and in 1950 received the Stalin Peace Prize from the Soviet government.
