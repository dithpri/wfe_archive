Location:
The Outback is a sprawling, arid region characterised by sparse vegetation and extreme temperatures.

Political Structure:
The region is home to numerous authoritarian dictatorships, featuring centralised governance, limited civil liberties, and strict law enforcement.

Climate:
The climate is predominantly arid, with long, hot summers and short, mild winters. Annual rainfall is less than 250 mm.

Tourism:
Tourism is limited and mainly restricted to government-controlled sites. Independent travel is strongly discouraged.

Cultural Highlights:
While traditional music and crafts still exist, they are under tight state control.
