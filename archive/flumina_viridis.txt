The River shifts, creating new sandbars and oxbow lakes. On the sandbars, cottonwood saplings attempt to anchor themselves on the shifting sands. A blue heron gently strides in the shallows, unaware that the shoals of shad and sunfish took the easy path downstream. Along the river, mighty oak trees hold the soil together. Under their omnipresent reach, bloodroots open their petals to the beams of light leaking through the canopy. The wind blows, making the canopy sing, the leaves in an eternal rustling. The River is always shifting, the River is always breathing.
