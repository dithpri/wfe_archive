The Warsaw Pact, formally the Treaty of Friendship, Co-operation, and Mutual Assistance (Russian: &amp;#1044;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1075;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1074;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1088; &amp;#1086; &amp;#1076;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1091;&amp;#1078;&amp;#1073;&amp;#1077;, &amp;#1089;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1091;&amp;#1076;&amp;#1085;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1095;&amp;#1077;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1074;&amp;#1077; &amp;#1080; &amp;#1074;&amp;#1079;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1084;&amp;#1085;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1081; &amp;#1087;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1084;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1097;&amp;#1080;) and sometimes, informally, WarPac. was a collective defense treaty among the FUSR and other Soviet satellite states. The Warsaw Pact was created in reaction to the integration of West Germany into NATO in 1955 per the Paris Pacts of 1954, but it is also considered to have been motivated by Soviet desires to maintain control over military forces in Central and Eastern Europe.
