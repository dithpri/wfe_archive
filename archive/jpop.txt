The term J-pop (often stylized in all caps; an abbreviated form of &quot;Japanese popular music&quot;), refers to a form of popular music that entered the musical mainstream of Japan in the 1990s. In Japan it is known simply as pops (&amp;#12509;&amp;#12483;&amp;#12503;&amp;#12473;, poppusu). Modern J-pop has its roots in traditional music of Japan, and global 1960s pop and rock music. J-pop replaced kay&amp;#333;kyoku (&quot;Lyric Singing Music&quot;), a term for Japanese popular music from the 1920s to the 1980s in the Japanese music scene.[2]
