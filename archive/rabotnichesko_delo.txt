Rabotnichesko delo (Bulgarian: &amp;#1056;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1073;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1085;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1095;&amp;#1077;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1082;&amp;#1086; &amp;#1076;&amp;#1077;&amp;#1083;&amp;#1086;, &quot;Worker&#39;s Deed&quot;) was a Bulgarian daily newspaper that was the media organ of the Bulgarian Communist Party&#39;s Central Committee and was one of the People&#39;s Republic of Bulgaria&#39;s highest-circulation newspapers. The newspaper was established in 1927 and was issued from Sofia. The first newspaper was released on 5 March 1927. The paper was renamed to Duma (&quot;Word&quot;) in 1990. Duma, despite some financial troubles, is still issued today.
