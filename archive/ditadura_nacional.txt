The [b]Ditadura Nacional[/b] was the name of the Portuguese regime initiated by the election of President &Oacute;scar Carmona in 1928 (following all of the turbulence derived from the 28th May 1926 coup d&amp;#39;&eacute;tat, period which is named Ditadura Militar) that lasted until the adoption of the new constitution in 1933, when the r&eacute;gime changed its name to Estado Novo (New State). It is considered that the Ditadura Nacional, together with the Estado Novo, forms the historical period of the Portuguese Second Republic.[hr]
