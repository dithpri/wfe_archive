It is an international organization consisting of 55 African countries. The Union was established on July 9, 2002, forming the successor to the Organization of African Unity. The most important decisions of the Union are taken at a semi-annual meeting of heads of state and government representatives of member states through the so-called General Assembly of the African Union. The headquarters of the General Secretariat and the African Union Commission are located in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. In the February 2009 meeting of the Union&#146;s General Assembly chaired by Libyan leader Muammar Gaddafi, he announced the dissolution of the African Union Commission and the establishment of the AU Authority.
