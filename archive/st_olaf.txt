A Norwegian farming settlement in Minnesota, it was according to Rose, &quot;founded by the man who came up with the idea of canning tuna in its own juices&quot; named Heinrich von Andredunen who was celebrated every year with a parade of townsfolk dressed as cans of tuna and jars of mayonnaise. Its population, she related, could all be traced back to the same brother and sister, and are required by law to sign a pledge at the age of 15 to promise they will not do anything &quot;wild, crazy and impetuous,&quot; principally to prevent people painting their houses strange colors.
