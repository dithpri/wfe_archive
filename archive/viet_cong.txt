The Viet Cong (Vietnamese: Vi&amp;amp;#7879;t c&amp;amp;#7897;ng ( listen)), or National Liberation Front (NLF), was a political organization and army in South Vietnam and Cambodia that fought the United States and South Vietnamese governments during the Vietnam War (1959&#150;1975), and emerged on the winning side. It had both guerrilla and regular army units, as well as a network of cadres who organized peasants in the territory it controlled.
