The [i]Southern Pacific Allied Union[/i] (SPAU) is a regional alliance of 17 nations dedicated to cooperation, prosperity, and peace across the Pacific Rim. Founded on the principles of freedom of movement, open trade, and mutual support, SPAU functions as an integrated political and economic community, tailored for the unique geography, cultures, and challenges of the South Pacific.

Formally established in 2009, SPAU facilitates seamless travel, residency, and employment between member nations, eliminates trade tariffs within the bloc, and promotes shared development in fields such as climate resilience, digital infrastructure, public health, and education.
