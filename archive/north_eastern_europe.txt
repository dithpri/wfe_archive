North eastern Europe is a region made up of twelve countries. The region was founded in 1915 by the kingdom of Esthervania which is by far the largest nation in the region. North eastern Europe is inhabited by the Slavic peoples of the area which are divided into ten different ethnic groups. These being Aeromanians, Borisgovans, Damselvanians, Esthervanians, Fraustravians, Krokazhians, Kroslavs, Lebdovans, Litvinburgers, Souvlakians and Vladoslavs. Each of the twelve nations have their own languages but being all former soviet nations Russian is also an official language and taught in schools throughout the region.
