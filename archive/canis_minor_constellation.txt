[i]Canis Minor is a small constellation in the northern celestial hemisphere. In the second century, it was included as an asterism, or pattern, of two stars in Ptolemy&#39;s 48 constellations, and it is counted among the 88 modern constellations. Its name is Latin for &quot;lesser dog&quot;, in contrast to Canis Major, the &quot;greater dog&quot;; both figures are commonly represented as following the constellation of Orion the hunter.[/i][b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025.[/b]
