[b]Initially an artificial island construction venture crowdfunded by twitchy cappuccino-sipping armchair libertarians and engineering nerds, Nationland is now constitutes a fantastic case study for micronation arcology and cautionary tale against trusting college graduates with 3D models and charming infographics.

As far as massive oceanic fields of concrete slabs go, Nationland is relatively habitable, or at least geologically stable[color=snuggles]*[/color]. The real draw, of course, is the lack of international oversight - that, and the consistently calm weather, ascribed to an exodus of faintly horrified chthonic deities. [/b]
