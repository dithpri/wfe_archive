The &quot;[b]Old World[/b]&quot; (Latin: [i]Mundus Vetus[/i]) is a term for Afro-Eurasia that originated in Europe c.&amp;#8201;1596, after Europeans had become aware of the existence of the Americas. It is used to contrast the continents of Africa, Europe, and Asia in the Eastern Hemisphere, previously thought of by their inhabitants as comprising the entire world, with the &quot;New World&quot;, a term for the newly encountered lands of the Western Hemisphere, particularly the Americas
