[b]The Congress of Vienna in 1815 reshaped Europe, establishing the United Kingdom of the Netherlands under William I of the House of Orange, uniting the Netherlands, Belgium, and Luxembourg as a buffer state against France. The north thrived on shipping and trade, while the south prospered through industry and mining; however, deep religious, linguistic, and economic divisions led to growing tensions between north and south, ultimately laying the groundwork for Belgium&#146;s independence in 1830.[/b]
