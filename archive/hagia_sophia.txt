Hagia Sophia (lit. &quot;Holy Wisdom&quot;) is a late antique place of worship in Istanbul, Turkey, designed by the Greek geometers Isidore of Miletus and Anthemius of Tralles. Built in 537 as the patriarchal cathedral of the imperial capital of Constantinople, it was the largest Christian church of the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire and the Eastern Orthodox Church.

[i]Refounded June 17, 2022 to safeguard against invasion.[/i]
[b]The Hagia Sophia is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It will remain locked to prevent vandalism.[/b]
