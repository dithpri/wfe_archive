The Guianas, sometimes called by the Spanish loan-word [b]Guayanas[/b], are a region in north-eastern South America which includes the following three territories

&#149; French Guiana, an overseas department of France

&#149; Guyana, known as British Guiana from 1831 until 1966, after the colonies of Berbice, Essequibo, and Demerara, taken from the Netherlands in 1814, were merged into a single colony

&#149; Suriname, until 1814 a part of Dutch Guiana, together with Berbice, Essequibo and Demerara. &#151;Wikipedia[hr]
