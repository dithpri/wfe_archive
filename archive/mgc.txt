[b]Megestrol caproate[/b], abbreviated as [b]MGC[/b], also known as megestrol 17&amp;#945;-caproate, as well as 17&amp;#945;-hydroxy-6-dehydro-6-methylprogesterone 17&amp;#945;-caproate or as 17&amp;#945;-hydroxy-6-methylpregna-4,6-diene-3,20-dione 17&amp;#945;-caproate, is a synthetic pregnane steroid and a derivative of progesterone and 17&amp;#945;-hydroxyprogesterone. It is the C17&amp;#945; caproate (hexanoate) ester of megestrol. Closely related medications include megestrol acetate (MGA; megestrol 17&amp;#945;-acetate), acetomepregenol (megestrol 3&amp;#946;,17&amp;#945;-diacetate), and cymegesolate (megestrol 17&amp;#945;-acetate 3&amp;#946;-cypionate).In addition to MGA, analogues of MGC include chlormadinone caproate, gestonorone caproate, hydroxyprogesterone caproate, medroxyprogesterone caproate, and methenmadinone caproate.
