[color=#FF0000][b]NORTHERN REDLANDS[/b][/color]

[color=#888888][b]INTRODUCTION[/b][/color]
The [b]Northern Redlands[/b] is a large region covered in tall forests, rolling mountains, and pure-white sand beaches that is home to fifteen distinct nations. The Encabo River, which flows through the Valley of Death, has carved a massive canyon towards the southernmost parts. To the east, Butcher Lake serves as the largest and deepest lake in the region, with an area of 27,420 square miles. Mt. Mik in the northwest serves as the tallest mountain the region.
