[region]Krefeld[/region]  is a city in [region]North Rhine Westphalia[/region], Germany. It is located northwest of D&uuml;sseldorf, its centre lying just a few kilometres to the west of the River Rhine. On 21 June 1943 British bombs destroyed large parts of east of the city; a firestorm consumed most of the city center apart from the central train station, which remained intact apart from minor damage. Restitution from the [region]Bank of England[/region] treasury will increase domestic capital by 1,000,000 Marks per year to be used in infrastructure and re-development.

City and boroughs are restricted to ethnic Germans pertaining to Laws of Citizenship and Integration within the Reich [/b]
