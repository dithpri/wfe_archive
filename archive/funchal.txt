Funchal is the largest city, the municipal seat and the capital of Portugal&amp;#39;s Autonomous Region of Madeira. The city has a population of 111,892[1] and has been the capital of Madeira for more than five centuries. Funchal is located inside a natural amphitheater-shaped valley, with gentle slopes beginning at the coast which rise to 1200 meters, that provided a natural shelter for early settlers.
In addition to the urbanized area, the municipality includes the Ilhas Selvagens.

Official name: Concelho do Funchal
Name origin: funcho, Portuguese for fennel
