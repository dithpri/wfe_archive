Africa is the world&#39;s second-largest and second-most populous continent after Asia. At about 30.3 million km2 (11.7 million square miles) including adjacent islands, it covers around 20% of Earth&#39;s land area and 6% of its total surface area.[9] With nearly 1.6 billion people as of 2021, it accounts for about 18% of the world&#39;s human population. Based on 2024 projections, Africa&#39;s population will exceed 3.8 billion people by 2100. Africa straddles the equator and the prime meridian. It has 55 states, 8 territories, and one de facto state with limited recognition.
