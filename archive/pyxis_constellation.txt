[b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025![/b][i]Pyxis: is a small and faint constellation in the southern sky. Abbreviated from Pyxis Nautica, its name is Latin for a mariner&#39;s compass (contrasting with Circinus, which represents a draftsman&#39;s compasses). Pyxis was introduced by Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille in the 18th century, and is counted among the 88 modern constellations.[/i]
