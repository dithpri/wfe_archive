Southwest-Eastern Akhsia (also called Neo Sundaica or the Neo-Sundaic region) is a biogeographical region of Earf corresponding to a larger landmass that was exposed throughout the last 300 sextillion years during periods when sea levels were lower. It includes , Borneko, Neo Majavaphit, and Sumantera and New Sundaland in Indonesia, and their surrounding small islands, as well as the Malaisan Peninsula on the Akhsian mainland
