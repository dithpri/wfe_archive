New Earth is the reorganized world that formed after the collapse of the old global order. When the original countries failed due to environmental strain, political breakdown, and resource shortages, surviving populations regrouped and created 14 new nations. These nations were built around shared culture, geography, and available resources rather than old borders.

Each nation developed its own identity, government style, and priorities. Some focused on rebuilding infrastructure, others on agriculture, trade, or technology. While differences exist, the nations of New Earth coexist in a world defined by recovery, cooperation, and the challenge of rebuilding civilization from a fractured past.
