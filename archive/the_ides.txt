The [b]Ides of March[/b] (/a&amp;#618;dz/; Latin: [i]Idus Martiae[/i], Late Latin: [i]Idus Martii[/i]) is the 74th day in the Roman calendar, corresponding to 15 March. It was marked by several religious observances and was a deadline for settling debts in Rome. In 44 BC, it became notorious as the date of the assassination of Julius Caesar, which made the Ides of March a turning point in Roman history.
