Shia Islam is the second largest branch of Islam after Sunni Islam. Shias adhere to the teachings of Prophet Muhammad (saww) and the religious guidance of his progeny, The Ahl al-Bayt (a.s.). Shia Muslims account for approximately 15 percent of the total Muslim population in the world. Shiism has the greatest influence in the contemporary world in Iran where nearly 90 percent of Muslims are Shiite, but Shia are also the majority in Iraq, Bahrain, Lebanon, and Yemen.

This is a region founded by and for Shiite Muslims, whether Jafari, Zaydi, Alawite, Alevi or Ismaili
