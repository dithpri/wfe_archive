The Vereenigde Oostindische Compagnie or in contemporary spelling United East India Company, abbreviated to VOC (1602-1800), was a private Dutch trading company with a monopoly on overseas trade between the Republic of the Seven United Provinces and the area east of the Cape of Good Hope and west of the Magellan Strait.
