Kharis and Horai was once a singular nation consisting of a sizeable archipelago and a region of mainland coastline cut off from the bulk of the continent by a mountain range. In the last century, the citizens, known as Beloveds, opted to separate into three different entities.
They are considered sister countries who still maintain strong ties to each other, and the name of the original country is preserved in the region&#39;s name and in each country&#39;s capital city. While the regions vary in policy on a lower level, major policies such as freedom of marriage and abolishment of the death penalty are held region-wide.
While all genders are afforded the same rights and opportunities, women by custom make up the majority of leaders.
