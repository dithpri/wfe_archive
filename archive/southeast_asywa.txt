Southeast Asywa lies on the historic peninsula of Survarnapa, the &#147;Golden Land,&#148; a region long shaped by trade, migration, and the rise and fall of empires. Ancient kingdoms once ruled the fertile river plains before waves of conquest and colonial influence from the Iberasque, Parafranz, and Druddic empires reshaped the region. Today the region is dominated by the Armed Republic of Arumia, a nation rebuilt from decades of conflict and genocide. Despite its turbulent past, Southeast Asywa remains a crossroads of cultures, languages, and histories.
