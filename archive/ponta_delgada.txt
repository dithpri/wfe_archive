Ponta Delgada is a city and municipality on the island of S&atilde;o Miguel in the archipelago of the Azores, an autonomous region of Portugal. It includes 44,403 residents in the urban area, and approximately 20,113 inhabitants in the three central parishes that comprise the historical city: S&atilde;o Pedro, S&atilde;o Sebasti&atilde;o, S&atilde;o Jos&eacute;.

Following the revised constitution of 1976, Ponta Delgada is the administrative capital of the Autonomous Region of the Azores (the Regional Assembly is located in Horta, Faial, while the judiciary and the Roman Catholic diocese are located in Angra do Hero&iacute;smo, Terceira).
