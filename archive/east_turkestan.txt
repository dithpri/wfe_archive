East Turkestan (Uyghur: &amp;#1588;&amp;#1749;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1602;&amp;#1609;&amp;#1610; &amp;#1578;&amp;#1736;&amp;#1585;&amp;#1603;&amp;#1609;&amp;#1587;&amp;#1578;&amp;#1575;&amp;#1606;, &amp;#1064;&amp;#1241;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1179;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1081; &amp;#1058;&amp;#1199;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1082;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1085;, Sh&amp;#601;rqiy T&uuml;rkistan) also known as Eastern Turkistan, Chinese Turkestan, Uyghurstan, Uyghuristan is a political term with multiple meanings depending on context and usage. Historically, the term was invented by Russian Turkologists like Nikita Bichurin in the 19th century to replace the term Chinese Turkestan, which referred to the Tarim Basin in the southwestern part of Xinjiang province of the Qing dynasty.[hr]
