Togo, a nation on West Africa&#146;s Gulf of Guinea, is known for its palm-lined beaches and hilltop villages. Koutammakou, inhabited by the Batammariba people, is a traditional settlement of fortresslike clay huts dating to the 17th century. In the capital Lom&eacute; are the multistory Grand March&eacute; bazaar and the Fetish Market, offering traditional talismans and remedies relating to the vodun religion (aka voodoo).
