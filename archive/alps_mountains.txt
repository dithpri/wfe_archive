The Alps (/&aelig;lps/; French: Alpes [alp]; German: Alpen [&amp;#712;alpn&amp;#809;]; Italian: Alpi [&amp;#712;alpi]; Romansh: Alps; Slovene: Alpe [&amp;#712;&aacute;&amp;#720;lp&amp;#603;]) are the highest and most extensive mountain range system that lies entirely in Europe,[2][note 1] separating Southern from Central and Western Europe and stretching approximately 1,200 kilometres (750 mi) across eight Alpine countries (from west to east): France, Switzerland, Monaco, Italy, Liechtenstein, Austria, Germany, and Slovenia
