The Empyrean Isles were discovered in 1964 by pioneer Herbie Hancock. The geopolitical region now encompasses a large central archipelago and a number of outlying islands and atoll reefs. The largest and most densely populated island is Cantaloupe Island. 

The regions sub-tropical climate and areas of outstanding natural beauty makes it a popular tourist destination and the rainforests of the Oliloqui Valley and around the Mayai, the largest mountain on Cantaloupe Island, attract numerous visitors each year.
