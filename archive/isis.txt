The Islamic State of Iraq and Syria (ISIS), also known as ISIL or Daesh, is a radical Sunni jihadist group that seeks to establish a global caliphate under a strict Sharia law. Originally an offshoot of Al-Qaeda in Iraq, it gained international notoriety in 2014 by seizing vast territories across Iraq and Syria and declaring a &quot;state.&quot;
The group persists as a decentralised global network and is internationally designated as a terrorist group due to its history of mass casualties, human rights abuses, and genocidal campaigns against religious minorities.
