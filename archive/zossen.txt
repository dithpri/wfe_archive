[b][region]Zossen[/region] is a quiet town in Brandenburg, about 30 kilometers south of [region]Berlin City State[/region], and next to the B96 highway. After World War II the area became the site of a Soviet military camp in East Germany, the largest outside Russia, housing as many as 75,000 Soviet men, women and children with daily trains going to Moscow, until Soviet troops pulled out in August 1994.[/b]
