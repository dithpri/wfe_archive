[b]An ancient region of Europe, corresponding to modern France, Belgium, the southern Netherlands, 
south-western Germany, and northern Italy. The area south of the Alps was conquered in 222 BC 
by the Romans, who called it Cisalpine Gaul. The area north of the Alps, known as Transalpine Gaul, 
was taken by Julius Caesar between 58 and 51 BC.[/b]
