The islands of Suthurland is small and fjordish. Its longest fjord is 373 kilometres long (232 miles). The regional capital is not yet claimed, but is discussed to be N&yacute;a Reykjav&iacute;kurh&ouml;fn, with a population of 4 million (11 million metropolitan). It is situated in between the Faroe Islands and Iceland, and is said to be the old mythical Thule Island, even though its highly doubted amongst geologists and geographs. The region is for the most an isolationist region, but is a quite good visited region, as it is famous for colourful aurora borealis and its high population of European wildcats.
