The mountains are rugged and imposing, forming a natural barrier between the Caspian Sea to the Anatolian plateau. These ranges are not only significant geographical features but also cultural and historical divides. These mountains have served as refuges for communities during periods of invasion and conflict, and their highlands are home to rich biodiversity. The mountainous terrain also contributes to the distinct climatic zones, with cooler, wetter conditions in the highlands contrasting with the arid lowlands.
