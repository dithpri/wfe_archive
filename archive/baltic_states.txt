[b][i][url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Baltic-states]Baltic States[/url], northeastern region of Europe containing the countries of [url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Estonia]Estonia[/url], [url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Latvia]Latvia[/url], and [url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Lithuania]Lithuania[/url], on the eastern shores of the Baltic Sea. The Baltic states are bounded on the west and north by the Baltic Sea, on the southeast by Belarus, and on the southwest by Poland. The Russian [url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Kaliningrad]Kaliningrad[/url] Oblast is situated between Poland and Lithuania on the Baltic Sea.[/i]

Estonian, Latvian and Lithuanian nationalists are welcome. A single party unicameral political system is in place with 1/3 of seats available for each nationality[/b]
