The [b]Cooperative Council for the States of Southern and Central Asia[/b], colloquially known as the [b]Middle Asian Council[/b], is a regional intergovernmental political and economic union consisting of six states in southern and central Asia: [nation]Eran-e Bozorg[/nation], [nation]Kroraine[/nation], [nation]Taxarstan[/nation], [nation]Bod Chenpo[/nation], [nation]Karduene[/nation], and [nation]Aryavarta Bharata[/nation]. The Charter of the Middle Asian Council is to be signed on February 9th, 2019, after which the union will formally be established.

Of the member states: four of the six are monarchies, one of which is an absolute monarchy; one is a unitary republic; and one is a federal republic.
