Six nations comprise the Eurasian Empire - the largest of which, the Russian Territories, administers the region.  The Duchy of Finland, the Polish States, the Crimean Khaganate, and the Kazakh and Mongolian Khanates are protectorate states of the Russian Territories - together they are administered by the Parliament of the Eurasian Empire.  The Parliament consists of the General Assembly, Council of Ministers, and the Sovereign Quorum.

The General Assembly and Council of Ministers are comprised of members elected every three and six years, respectively.  The Sovereign Quorum consists of the Imperial Council in concert with the Sovereign - together they constitute the quorum necessary to administer the parliament.
