[i]Circinus is a small, faint constellation in the southern sky, first defined in 1756 by the French astronomer Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille. Its name is Latin for compass, referring to the drafting tool used for drawing circles (it should not be confused with Pyxis, a constellation that represents a mariner&#39;s compass which points north).[/i][b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025.[/b]
