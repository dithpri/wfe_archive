The fundamental principle of democracy is equality. Each person is of equal worth on the basis of common membership in the human family. This is the political principle that claims the hearts of the world&#146;s people. 

The fundamental principle of the market is capital. Each person&#146;s worth depends on an unequal ability to extract money from the economy. This is the economic principle that leads to the hunt for individual success and canonization of the rich, and derision for the poor unwashed masses. 

These two principles are at odds. 

Either we are social allies, united by our humanity and common needs, or we are social enemies, divided by our competition over jobs, houses, and food. 

Eventually, each of us must choose&#133;
