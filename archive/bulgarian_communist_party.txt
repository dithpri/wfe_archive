The&nbsp;Bulgarian Communist Party&nbsp;(BCP;&nbsp;Bulgarian:&nbsp;&amp;#1041;&amp;#1098;&amp;#1083;&amp;#1075;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1082;&amp;#1072; &amp;#1050;&amp;#1086;&amp;#1084;&amp;#1091;&amp;#1085;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1095;&amp;#1077;&amp;#1089;&amp;#1082;&amp;#1072; &amp;#1055;&amp;#1072;&amp;#1088;&amp;#1090;&amp;#1080;&amp;#1103; (&amp;#1041;&amp;#1050;&amp;#1055;),&nbsp;romanized:&nbsp;Balgarska Komunisticheska Partiya (BKP) was the&nbsp;Communist&nbsp;and&nbsp;Marxist-Leninist&nbsp;ruling party&nbsp;of the&nbsp;People&#39;s Republic of Bulgaria&nbsp;from 1946 until 1989 when the country ceased to be a&nbsp;socialist state. The Bulgarian Communist Party had dominated the&nbsp;Fatherland Front&nbsp;coalition that took power in 1944, late in&nbsp;World War II, after it led a&nbsp;coup&nbsp;against Bulgaria&#39;s tsarist regime in conjunction with the&nbsp;Red Army&#39;s crossing the border. It controlled its armed forces, the&nbsp;Bulgarian People&#39;s Army.
