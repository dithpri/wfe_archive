[b]Realm of the [region]Empire of Persia[/region][/b]
[hr]The [b]Achaemenid Empire[/b], founded by Cyrus the Great in 550 BCE, was the largest empire of ancient history at its peak, stretching from the Balkans to the Indus Valley. It was defined by a sophisticated administrative system of satrapies, the standardized Royal Road, and a groundbreaking policy of cultural and religious tolerance toward conquered peoples. Under iconic rulers like Darius the Great and Xerxes, the empire flourished as a center of Zoroastrianism and monumental architecture, most notably at Persepolis.
