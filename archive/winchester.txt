[region]Winchester[/region] the First Capital of England is a city in the county of Hampshire, on the edge of England&#39;s South Downs National Park. In 871 CE Alfred was crowned King of Wessex and established Winchester as his capital. The city was named in the time of the Kingdom of Wessex, Venta Belgarum became Venta Caester then Wintancaester and eventually corrupted to Winchester. Prior to the Saxon era, The Great Hall of Winchester Castle houses the medieval round table linked to King Arthur. Before Roman occupation, Winchester was a Celtic Belgae town settled prior to 150 BCE.[/color][/b]
