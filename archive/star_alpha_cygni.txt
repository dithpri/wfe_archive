Deneb, also designated as &amp;#945; Cygni (alpha Cygni), is a variable and double supergiant star of spectral class A2 in the constellation of Cygnus. Deneb visual magnitude is 1.25, making it the 18th brightest star in the sky. Because of its brightness, Deneb is clearly visible to the naked eye when observed from locations with dark skies, and should be also quite easily visible from light polluted areas.
