[b]Usedom[/b] (German: [i]Usedom[/i] [&amp;#712;u&amp;#720;z&amp;#601;d&amp;#596;m], Polish: [i]Uznam[/i] [&amp;#712;uznam]) is a Baltic Sea island in Pomerania, divided between Germany and Poland. It is the second largest Pomeranian island after R&uuml;gen, and the most populous island in the Baltic Sea.

With an annual average of 1,906 hours of sunshine, Usedom is the sunniest region of both Germany and Poland, and it is also one of the sunniest islands in the Baltic Sea,[1] hence its nickname &quot;Sun Island&quot; (German: [i]Sonneninsel[/i], Polish: [i]Wyspa S&amp;#322;o&amp;#324;ca[/i]).
