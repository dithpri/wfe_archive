From 927 to 1707, the [b]Kingdom of England[/b] was a sovereign state to the northwest of Europe. At its height, [b]England[/b] spanned the southern two-thirds of the island of [b]Great Britain[/b] &amp; several smaller islands. [b]England[/b] as a state began on 12 July 927 AD after a gathering of kings from throughout Britain at Eamont Bridge, Cumbria, but broadly traces its origins to the Anglo-Saxon settlement of [b]Britain [/b]&amp; the petty states that followed &amp; ultimately united.
