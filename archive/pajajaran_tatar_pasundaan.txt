Traces its origins to the ancient kingdom of Pajajaran, which collapsed in 1579 during the expansion of Islamic polities. Sundanese lineages fled into the mountains and dispersed, preserving their genealogies in secrecy for 357 years. During the colonial era, clandestine Pasundaan societies evolved into organized networks that reunited these descendants. In 1936, they proclaimed the restoration of Pasundaan under the Arkakala, establishing an absolute monarchy grounded in dynastic legitimacy and the revival of Sunda identity.
