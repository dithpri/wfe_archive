[b]Vatican City[/b], officially [b]Vatican City State[/b] (Italian: [i]Stato della Citt&agrave; del Vaticano[/i]; Latin: [i]Status Civitatis Vaticanae[/i]), is an independent city-state enclaved within Rome, Italy. Established with the Lateran Treaty (1929), it is distinct from, yet under &quot;full ownership, exclusive dominion, and sovereign authority and jurisdiction&quot; of the Holy See (Latin: [i]Sancta Sedes[/i]). With an area of 44 hectares (110 acres), and a population of about 1,000, it is the smallest sovereign state in the world by both area and population.
