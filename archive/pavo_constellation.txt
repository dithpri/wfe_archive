[b]Founded by the andarian starfleet on: 04/05/2025![/b][i]Pavo: is a constellation in the southern sky whose name is Latin for &#39;peacock&#39;. Pavo first appeared on a 35-cm (14 in) diameter celestial globe published in 1598 in Amsterdam by Petrus Plancius and Jodocus Hondius and was depicted in Johann Bayer&#39;s star atlas Uranometria of 1603, and was likely conceived by Plancius from the observations of Pieter Dirkszoon Keyser and Frederick de Houtman. French explorer and astronomer Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille gave its stars Bayer designations in 1756.[/i]
