Tranco is divided central and north by the Pennines, a low mountain range that stretches for almost 250 miles (400 km) In the far northwest corner of the country, the highest point in Tranco,Fairbell, stands at 978 m (3210 ft). With a few exceptions, the balance of Tranco&#146;s land, central and south, is a series of rolling hills, punctuated by undulating lowlands and fields with the presences of forests. In the east-central region of the country the area is flat low-lying marshland drained for agricultural use. The lowest point in the country is found there, at -4 meters below sea level. .
